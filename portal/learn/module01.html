Module 01

Mathematics for Machine Learning

The invisible engine that powers every prediction, optimization, and decision in Data Science.

1. Linear Algebra: The Language of Data

Why do we need this?

Computers see multi-dimensional matrices. Linear Algebra allows us to treat a dataset of 1,000,000 rows as a single object.

Intuition: Coordinates in Space

Imagine a house as a vector: $\mathbf{x} = [3, 1500, 2]^T$. Linear Algebra is the math of finding patterns among these points.

Test Your Understanding

If weight vector $\mathbf{w} = [0.5, 0.5]$ and input $\mathbf{x} = [10, 20]$, what is the prediction score?

Answer: $0.5(10) + 0.5(20) = \mathbf{15}$.

2. Calculus: The Engine of Optimization

Why do we need this?

Calculus tells us exactly how to change a model's weights to reduce its error.

Intuition: The Blind Hiker

The Gradient is the slope under your feet, telling you which direction to step to reach the valley (minimum error).

Test Your Understanding

If slope is positive, should you increase or decrease weight?

Answer: Decrease.

Learning Outcome

  • ✓ Visualize data as vectors in space.
  • ✓ Understand why "The Gradient" is the compass of learning.
