Capstone Project: Customer Churn Prediction

You are provided with a multi-table dataset from a major service provider. Your goal is to predict which customers are likely to Churn (leave the service) in the next 30 days.

The Intuition: The Final Boss

This isn't just a coding exercise. It is a simulation of the chaos, stress, and strategic decision-making required to reach the top 1% of a real Kaggle competition. You must manage your time between feature engineering and model ensembling.

The Data Blueprint

  • profiles.csv: User demographics, tenure, and ZIP codes.
  • transactions.csv: Granular spending history (needs aggregation!).
  • target.csv: The ground truth labels (Training set only).

Avoid the Common Traps

This dataset has been "sabotaged" with standard competition artifacts.

1. The Drift Trap

The Age distribution in the test set is shifted by +10 years compared to the training set. If your model relies too heavily on raw Age, your private leaderboard score will plummet. Use Adversarial Validation to detect this.

2. The High-Cardinality Leak

The ZIP_Code column contains 250 unique values. If you Target Encode this globally, you will experience extreme leakage. Implement OOF Smoothed Encoding strictly inside your folds.

3. Redundant Features

We've added features with 0.99 correlation. Use an Automated Pruning Loop to remove them and speed up your training.

Final Submission Checklist

  • Is your CV loop Stratified 5-Fold?
  • Did you blend at least two models (LGBM + XGB)?
  • Did you scan for the optimal F1 threshold?
  • Is your final submission file in the exact required format?
